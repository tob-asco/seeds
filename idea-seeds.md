## evolutionary programming
- CreatorName: tobi
- Title: evolutionary programming
- Slogan: Make Code Just Like Evolution Made Our Brain
- {Nowadays, we create code that aims to mimic our brain while using the resources of a CPU: Artificial Intelligence.
Evolutionary Programming, on the other hand, tries to mimic How Our Brain Was Made.

We want to imitate the evolutionary driver "better => survival" for software, so we choose a definition for "surviving code" and "dying code", based on a defined "goal".
We modularize our code, so we can keep modules constant that "survived" and change the "dying" ones.
Then we randomly change our modules by virtue of huge calculation power and simply let our code develop itself, i.e. we create the "fittest" code.}

## Eiertür
- CreatorName: tobi
- Title: egg timer window closer
- Slogan: Close Your Window After Chosen Time
- {Attachment gadget to automatically close the open window with a time delay. W/o needed power supply.

Attach it to the window frame, e.g., next to the window hinge.
The rod is positioned away and acts as a lever.
The lever grips as far away from the hinge as possible on the frame or window.
The goal is for the rod to push the window closed via a tensioned spring.
The reason is that closing takes some time; otherwise, one could close it manually.

The delay is like an egg timer, with three options:
  - Starts closing immediately but as slowly as set.
  - Closes the window quite quickly only after the set time.
  - Combination: Use the last minute to close gently.

Why:
  To end ventilation after a reasonable time without having to think about closing.
  It's easier to think about ventilation itself (odor, habit, etc.).
  Closing is forgotten and consumes a lot of energy.
Where:
  Basically, at any window/door that opens on a hinge.
  Bathroom
  Bedroom windows (you can fall asleep without unintentionally venting all night).

Operation:
  - Close the window and 'calibrate' the spring (i.e., attach the lever to the frame).
  - Slightly over-tension the spring against the window seal pressure.
    (e.g., manually pre-tension with a ratchet, as strong as needed, depending on seal pressure)
  (- Adjust the spring force to also close heavy doors.)
  - Open the window to any desired width and tension the ratchet accordingly.
  - Set the duration by turning the egg timer.
  - When releasing, depending on the options (as above), start closing/ticking immediately.

Challenge:
  - Attachment of the device to the frame
    Suction cup: not very reliable, subjected to constant torque
    Screwing: may damage the seal, inflexible, devalues the window
    Gluing: inflexible, and constant torque is known to damage adhesives
    Combinations of the above ideas: bloated

    Above the hinge, a thin plate goes through the slot when the window is open:
    So, a double right angle that wraps around the frame
    lies equally on the outside and inside of the frame
    The hinge prevents the 'U-bracket' from slipping down
    Can be easily fastened in any direction and offers a surface for attachment

    Problem:
    Slightly damages the seal effect
    Different frame thicknesses require U-brackets of different widths

Variants:
  - Security variant:
    Attach a drop bolt on the opposite side of the frame (where it is open).
    Attach a pin to the door that protrudes in the door plane.
    Then, when the pin reaches the drop bolt at 95% closure, it triggers it.
    The drop bolt falls and holds the pin in place.
    Preventing external opening.

    Multiple pins and drop bolts on one door possible.
  - Installation variant:
    Attachment question not a problem.
}
- CreationTime: 2021-12-22
- Topics: autarkic solutions; reduction

## Geistentschlackung
- CreatorName: tobi
- Title: mental detox
- Slogan: Reset Your Habits By Starving Them
- {Like fasting for the physical organism, cleanse the mind through abstinence from:
Habits.
Mainly those with negative effects (sugar, laziness, Netflix, ..) but also neutral/positive ones possible.
Reason: Habituation happens slowly, so within the time span of good enough memory, you don't notice a difference. It is the driving force and the reason for a large part of our behavior. For example, "Time heals all wounds" can be attributed solely to it, not to time itself - time is just the fuel for habituation.
That's why breaking free from the current state of habituation through abrupt but prolonged changes in daily routine makes sense: You look at your life from the outside, as an objective observer. The mask of habituation doesn't cloud your view. Since habituation is a driver of addiction (the dose of the addictive substance increases with time - that is, with growing habituation), you can return to an earlier stage of addiction. Depending on the nature of the addiction (negative: drugs, positive: love), returning to the quantity before detoxification can create a more positive/negative feeling.}
